**Context and Primary Project Feature**  
The project is focused on designing, building, and testing an automated Lego sorting machine. Its primary purpose is to organize Lego kits used in EGR101 and EGR102 courses, as well as to recreate individual Lego kit bags for other sets. The system will use computer vision to identify parts and guide them into the correct bins, streamlining the process of preparing and maintaining Lego kits for both students and instructors.

**Scriptural/Biblical Impact / Christian Worldview Features**  
This project encourages stewardship of time and resources by reducing manual effort in organizing Lego kits, thereby allowing students and faculty to focus more on learning and teaching. By applying skills in computer science and engineering toward a practical solution that serves the academic community, the project reflects the Christian value of using one’s talents to benefit others.

**Required Features**

* A computer vision algorithm capable of recognizing Lego pieces by shape, size, and color.  
* Integration of a Raspberry Pi and camera to capture and process part images in real-time.  
* Communication between the computer vision system and the mechanical/electrical sorting components to direct parts into bins.  
* Reliable and user-friendly operation for use in an academic setting.

**Additional Features**

* A dataset of Lego parts to improve classification accuracy.  
* Functionality to process digital Lego instructions and re-bag kits according to original specifications.  
* Collaboration between computer science, mechanical, and electrical teams to ensure a cohesive system.

**Intended Deliverable**  
A fully functional, automated Lego sorting machine that can efficiently identify, classify, and organize Lego parts into bins, with the added ability to recreate original kit bags for course use.

**Impact of Project**  
The project will significantly reduce the time and effort required to prepare Lego kits, benefiting both instructors and students in foundational engineering courses. It will also demonstrate the power of interdisciplinary collaboration, combining computer science, mechanical design, and electrical engineering. More broadly, it provides students with valuable hands-on experience in applying machine learning, computer vision, and embedded systems to solve a real-world problem.
